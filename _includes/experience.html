Experiences

Scientific Director, Center for Genomics

2015 - present
Florida State University

As Director of the FSU Center for Genomics, I provide genomic data analysis services for researchers, run workshops on data generation and analysis, and consult on all aspects of genomic experiments, including experimental design. I lead the analysis of genomic data for many projects, including those that have utilized RNA-seq, ChIP-seq, MNase-seq, Repli-seq, de novo sequencing, and other types of data.

Graduate Research Assistant

2010 - 2014
Florida State University

My graduate training was under the mentorship of Hank Bass and Jonathan Dennis at FSU, and involved the analysis of chromatin structure dynamics in humans and maize using micrococcal nuclease as a probe of chromatin structure coupled with microarrays and sequencing. I developing an approach to profile nucleosome positioning, nucleosome sensitivity, and transcription factor binding simultaneously with MNase (DOI). I also was involved in the development of an approach to cost-effectively profile the chromatin landscapes in the human genome using targeted enrichment and illumina sequencing (DOI). Throughout the process of generating large-scale data sets, I self-trained in data analysis and programming in order to analyze the data I generated. As I learned to program, I developed user-friendly software in R to enable others to analyze and integrate different genomic data sets (http://github.com/dvera/travis).
